  • Suppose \(\operatorname{Pr}\left[X=x_{i}\right]=p_{i}\) but we think it’s from \(q_{i}\) and use correspondingly sized cede words

\[\begin{split} \begin{array}{r} \Rightarrow L(N)=-N \sum_{i} p_{i} \log q_{i} \text { and } \\ \frac{L-L_{\text {min }}}{N}=\sum_{i} p_{i} \log \left(\frac{p_{i}}{q_{i}}\right)=D_{K L}(\vec{p} \| \vec{q}) \end{array} \end{split}\]

KL (Rullback-Leibler) divergence.

Suppose we obtain samples \(\left\{X_{i}\right\}\) and want to decide whetter they are drawn from \(\vec{p}\) or \(\vec{q}\);

\[\begin{split} \begin{gathered} \log \left[\frac{P(\text { samples } \mid \vec{p})}{P(\text { samples } \mid \vec{q})}\right]=\sum_{i} n\left(x_{i}\right) \log 2\left(\frac{p_{i}}{q_{i}}\right) \\ \longrightarrow N \sum_{i} p_{i} \log \left(\frac{p_{i}}{q_{i}}\right)=N D_{k L}(\vec{p} \| \vec{q}) \end{gathered} \end{split}\]

\(\Rightarrow\) We need \(\gtrsim D_{K L}^{-1}(\vec{p} \| \vec{q})\) samples to distinguish between \(\vec{p}\) and \(\vec{q}\).

Mutual information

\(S(X) \cong\) lack of knowledge about \(X\).

Suppose we measure \(Y\), correlated with \(X_{0}\) ¿ By how much does our lack of knowledge?

\[\begin{split} \begin{gathered} =\int_{\text {entropy before }}(X)^{S} \begin{array}{l} S(X \mid Y)= \\ \text { expected } \\ \text { entropy after } \end{array} \\ =-\sum_{x} P_{X}(x) \log P_{X}(x)+\sum_{Y} P_{Y}(y) \sum_{x} P_{X}(x) \log P(x \mid y) \\ =\sum_{x, y} P(x, y) \log \left[\frac{P(y \mid x)}{P_{x}(x)}\right] \\ =\sum_{x, y} P(x, y) \log \left[\frac{P(x, y)}{P_{x}(x) P_{y}(y)}\right]=J(x ; y) \end{gathered} \end{split}\]
